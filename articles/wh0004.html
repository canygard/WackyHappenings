Tragic: Chris Pratt will still be playing Star Lord in Guardians of the Galaxy Volume 3

Another iconic character has been the victim of an unfortunate miscasting. When will Hollywood stop casting Chris Pratt as my favorite characters?

Damien Blockbuster October 31, 2021
Share this

Over the last few months, movie fans have been bombarded with unfortunate casting news. Nintendo fans are still coping with the tragic announcement from back in October that their favorite Italian plumber, Mario Mario, would be voiced by the Parks and Rec star Chris Pratt. Last week, the Garfield fanbase recieved similar news: The lasagna-consuming, Monday loathing cat we all know and love would also be destroyed at the hands of Pratt. It seems that Hollywood wants nothing more than to erase our childhoods and shove the Chris Pratt agenda down our throats, replacing our fond cartoon memories with empty Chris nonsense.

But now we have more to mourn than these animated casualties. In a recent interview with Marvel Studios president Kevin Feige, it was revealed that Pratt would be reprising his role as Peter Quill AKA Star Lord in the highly anticipated sequel, Guardians of the Galaxy Volume 3. That's right. If you thought superhero movies were safe from the Prattpocalypse, think again. "I grew up watching the Guardians of the Galaxy series," said a fan on the r/ChrisPrattHate subreddit. "Star Lord was one of my favorite characters in both movies. The way he portrayed the iconic character from the comics was incredible, it was like he leapt off the page. I'm heartbroken to hear that Chris Pratt will be coming back to taint my high opinion of the character."

"I just don't understand why we'd need to recast Quill," responded Feige. "Just because he was miscast in something else, we have to throw our character away? How are the two related?" The ignorant Marvel Studios president continues to apologize for the disgusting miscasting of Chris Pratt. It's clear that he, too, has been absorbed by the Pratt agenda. Attrocious.
