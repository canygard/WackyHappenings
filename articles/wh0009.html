Man forced to dismantle Jake Gyllenhaal shrine after girlfriend watches Taylor Swift's new short film

15 minutes after the release of Taylor Swift’s All Too Well   last night, one man lost a central decoration in his studio apartment.

Don TendreNovember 13, 2021
Share this

“He dropped her hand!” paraphrased longtime Swiftie Emma Vance. “What kind of person is so blinded by their loyalty to a celebrity that they would risk their relationship?” she asked reporters after threatening to end her 3 year relationship because of a 15 minute Taylor Swift ‘film’. “My boyfriend needs to get his shit together and stop turning a blind eye to the patriarchy.”

The boyfriend in question is Gale Anderson, movie buff and Jake Gyllenhaal superfan. “What can I say? I like the guy and his movies. Nightcrawler is pretty good and I liked him in Spider-Man.” After considering the idea of losing his girlfriend because of a little shrine, he decided to oblige her request. “I’m a little frustrated about it, I was quite happy with the shrine and she had no problem with it before.”

Anderson considers himself to be a casual Taylor Swift fan. Otherwise, as his girlfriend frequently reminds him, he would be single. “Yeah, I guess I like 1989. It’s a pretty good record. Don’t tell Emma, but I haven’t really listened to any of her other stuff.” When asked about the All Too Well, he claimed, “I haven’t seen the movie, but I did watch a commentary video about it on YouTube. Seems pretty dramatic to me. Didn’t the relationship happen like a decade ago?”

Whether you’re on team Swift or team Gyllenhaal, it’s safe to say that you can’t be neutral in this conflict. As short films about 10 year old, scandal-free celebrity relationships always do, All Too Well has divided the world. When this scenario inevitably happens to you, will you keep your shrine to Jake Gyllenhaal? Or surrender to the Taylor Swift majority?
