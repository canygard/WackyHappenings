Takuya Yamashiro feels left out after being excluded from events of Spider-Man: No Way Home

This article contains spoilers for Spider-Man: No Way Home.

Damien BlockbusterDecember 1, 2021
Share this

Before Spider-Man: No Way Home’s inciting incident, Takuya Yamashiro, better known as スパイダーマン (Supaidāman), was chilling in his mech, Leopardon, when suddenly, things started to get peculiar. Gone were his towering, cheaply costumed villains terrorizing the city. Gone were his machine guns and rocket launchers, and gone were the recycled web slinging and mech battling shots that tied together his daily routine. And in this universe, no one recognized him as Spider-Man.

In this universe, Spider-Man was an attractive British man pretending to be an attractive, stammering American man. Upon realizing this, Yamashiro set out to travel to New York to meet this impostor. In fact, he was hoping to make a friend. Before he could even secure plane tickets, however, he began to phase out of this reality, returning to his home dimension.

"When I suddenly shifted to this new universe, I was super hyped to show Tom Holland my cool mech. After Dr. Strange sent all of us Spider-Men home, though, I realized they had done the big fight without me! What the hell, guys!"

After he was sent home (despite the film’s title being “No Way Home”), Takuya Yamashiro went on a long, sad motorcycle ride to clear his head.
