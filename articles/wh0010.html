Sorry nerds, Tobey and Andrew won’t be in Spider-Man: No Way Home. Instead, you’re getting Tom Holland in some silly wigs

Although the film will contain alternate Spider-Men, the widespread rumors about Tobey Maguire and Andrew Garfield have been proven false. Tom Holland will be playing every Spider-Man, donning a different wig for each universe.

Damien BlockbusterNovember 17, 2021
Share this

Trailers for the latest installment in the MCU’s Spider-Man series have sent the Internet on fire with speculation and theories about the appearance of former Spider-Men, Andrew Garflied and Tobey Maguire. Due to the film’s multiversal premise, and confirmed performances from Alfred Melina (Doctor Octopus), Jamie Foxx (Electro), and Willem DeFoe (Green Goblin) among others, it seems natural to assume that these iconic villains would face off against their respective heroes.

The first trailer, which was released last night, all but confirmed these theories with a single line from Dr. Octopus, who, after Tom Holland’s mask is removed in battle, remarks “You’re not Peter Parker.” Because Dr. Octopus is not familiar with Tom Holland’s Peter Parker, many fans assumed that this was the same version of the character from Spider-Man 2 (2004). It would make sense that Otto Octavious would be confused by Tom Holland’s appearance if he had previously faced off against Tobey Maguire.

After dodging hundreds of questions about his involvement with the film, Tobey Maguire finally broke his silence, violating his nondisclosure agreement in an emotional revelation about the film. “I’m not in it. Don’t ask me one more goddamn time about this move. I’m not in it. The other Spider-Men are just Tom Holland in silly wigs. They couldn’t afford me.”

After confirming this leak on Twitter, Marvel Studios president, Kevin Feige had this to say:

“You losers really want to see these inferior, B-list Spider-Men corrupt our beautiful new Spider-Man franchise?” asked Feige. “There’s a reason their sequels were canceled. They’re trash. Tom Holland is what’s new, Tom Holland is what’s hot, and Tom Holland is Spider-Man in every fucking universe.”

In the film, the actor will don several silly, wacky, and comedic wigs to represent all of the different universes Spider-Man inhabits. Although many fans are shocked and upset about this revelation, I, for one, am relieved that everyone is going to shut up about this dumb comic book movie. The nerds will finally be silenced. I can rest.
