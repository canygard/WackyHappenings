HAPPENINGS

Opinion: Nothing happening lately has felt wacky, quirky, zany, or even slightly outlandish

All has been quiet in the once-whimsical world of Wacky Happenings.

Kim JeffersonNovember 22, 2021
Share this

“Sorry you haven’t seen much Wacky Happenings content in the last couple of days,” says Wacky Happenings founder and CEO, Connor Nygard. “I feel like I’m letting my followers down in a big way. I know that all 28 of you depend on this content for your fix of happenings that are wacky, stories that are offbeat, unfolding that are eccentric, and events that are madcap. It’s just that nothing of the sort has inspired me to write as of late.”

After closing the thesaurus.com tab on his laptop, he went on to apologize to his loyal followers. “I know there’s not a proper excuse to go five days without posting a single happening. I’ve kept you in the dark for over 120 cold, melancholy hours, and I apologize deeply. I never should have hurt you like that. I will do better.”

After dodging hundreds of questions about his involvement with the film, Tobey Maguire finally broke his silence, violating his nondisclosure agreement in an emotional revelation about the film. “I’m not in it. Don’t ask me one more goddamn time about this move. I’m not in it. The other Spider-Men are just Tom Holland in silly wigs. They couldn’t afford me.”

Although Nygard cites his hectic workload from school and work as a factor in the shortage of content, skeptics have speculated other nefarious shenanigans are to blame. “It’s because his girlfriend is in town,” explained a close friend of the mysterious news puppet master. “Yeah, right. This guy has a girlfriend,” sarcastically commented a hairless bystander. “And I’m Jeff Bezos.”

After tracking down his account on Steam, investigators have uncovered a much more likely explanation for the lack of happenings: His recent obsession with the 2017 video game, For Honor.
