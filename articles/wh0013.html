Spider-Man fan flames struggling movie theatre on Twitter due to inability to buy tickets

Ouch! This dying business was ratioed on Twitter. Buy more server space next time!

Damien BlockbusterNovember 29, 2021
Share this

When tickets became available for the highly anticipated Spider-Man sequel last night, movie theatre websites were flooded with nerds. Many became completely overwhelmed with traffic, displaying error messages and endless loading screens. With so many unhappy geeks seeing 404 errors, there was only one place for them to voice their complaints: Twitter.

The Twitter account for a chain of Utah movie theatres, the Larry H. Miller Megaplex, experienced the unfiltered wrath of these nerds firsthand. After a brutal and gut-wrenching series of insulting tweets from a user named @HumanSpider2201, one intern made the unfortunate mistake of responding.

"I'm just the social media guy!" protested Xander Miller, part of the social media team at the Utah-based chain of movie theatres. "I don't understand why everyone is being so nasty to me about this Spider-Man thing!"

In an attempt to broker peace, he replied to the complaints with a corporate apology message that would make his father, the owner of the chain, very proud. This attempt, however, was only met with more devastating insults. In-fact, the Megaplex Theatres account was ratioed.

Let this story be a lesson to any companies bold enough to venture into the wilderness of Twitter. Nver underestimate the sheer power of nerds.
