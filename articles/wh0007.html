Goodbye, Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson; Hello, Dwayne "The Rap" Johnson

An entire month after his appearance on Tech N9ne’s Face Off, the celebrity formerly known as Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson has finally given fans the genre-accurate name we’ve demanded.

John TendreNovember 8, 2021
Share this

If you’ve been a part of the Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson fan base for awhile now, you’re probably aware that most of the community has been extremely vocal about his inaccurate nickname. Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson, as we all know, has been a wrestler and a movie star, a heartthrob and a chiseled sculpture of a man, but has never made music in the rock and roll genre. For this reason, many fans have been embarrassed to publicly show their support for him.

“Whenever I tell someone I’m a Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson fan, I’m taking a huge risk,” says Twitter user @HugeBaldGuys. “People always ask me, ‘Oh, is that like a one man rock band?’ or ‘Big geology guy, eh?’ No. No no no. He’s a big hunky guy. A big hunky bald man. Big hunky bald man in action movies.” Further in the thread, the Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson fan went on to say “The barrage of questions never ends. I just never knew how to explain the guy to people. Even committed fans like us have to admit, his old nickname just never accurately described his career.”

On October 22nd, Zendaya fans flooded theatres to see their favorite actress in the dystopian sci-fi movie that had been in their feeds for months. Fans were beyond disappointed, however, to see that despite the movie posters, trailers, celebrity advertisements, Vogue photoshoots, and red carpet premieres, Zendaya appeared on screen for a mere 7 minutes at the end of the movie.

After unveiling his new moniker earlier today, Dwayne “The Rap” Johnson issued an apology for the years of confusion caused by his former title. “I’m sorry it took me this long to see the harm I was doing.” explained The Rap. “I never intended to mislead anyone about what I do. I hope that this new name will be a step in the right direction towards a more honest version of myself. I’m Dwayne ‘The Rap’ Johnson. I’m a rapper now. Dwayne ‘The Rap’ Johnson. I rap. I’m Dwayne ‘The Rap’ Johnson.”

I think I can speak for all Dwayne “The Rap” Johnson fans when I say that we forgive you. We forgive you for your actions, The Rap. We support and forgive you. We love you, Dwayne “The Rap” Johnson.
