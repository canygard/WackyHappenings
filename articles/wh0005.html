Unbelievable: Roommate on top floor blames noise complaint on upstairs neighbor

"That's insane, I was also woken up by that really dope guitar riff. The guy upstairs must be in a really good band or something. If you have a problem with it, I'd talk to the guy above me."

Axel StoneNovember 5, 2021
Share this

At 3:45 AM last night, room 304 tenants were abruptly awakened by a skull-piercing power chord produced by an electric guitar in the room above. After listening to one-third of a most deafening rendition of Say it Ain't So (as if there was a pleasant version), the residents had heard enough. “Maybe if he was decent at guitar, I’d let it slide,” One of the downstairs neighbors told reporters the next morning. “But this guy sucks.”

The guitarist responsible for this disturbance is 19 year old music major Stanley Shultz, the frontman of a grunge rock cover band. He claims that practicing at odd hours of the night gives him the inspiration he often lacks. As his roommate, I can confirm that he does, in fact, suck. What kind of creative juices do you need to make Weezer cover music? Asshole.

By the time apartment 404 occupants received a knock at the door, Schultz was already in the middle of Pink Triangle. After several rounds of knocking began to mess with his tempo, he decided to answer the door. According to eyewitnesses, Schultz acted completely dumbfounded, claiming that he, too, was disturbed by the music. As he suggested they try the tenant on the fifth floor, he struggled to keep his composure, beginning to let out a sick chuckle between words. The reason for this wicked laugh? There was no fifth floor in the building.

“Whew, we’re in the clear.” sighed Schultz, closing the door with one hand while hiding his guitar behind his back with the other. “Do you think they bought it?” No. Stanley. I don’t think they bought it. I actually think you’re a fucking dumbass.
